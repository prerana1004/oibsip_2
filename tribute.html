Savitribai Phule

1831- 1897

"First female Teacher of India"

>

Savitribai Phule was a teacher, Indian social reformer, educationalist, and poet from Maharashtra. Along with her husband, Jyotiba Phule in Maharashtra, she played a vital role in improving women's rights in India. She is cosidered to be the pioneer of India's feminist movement.

1831 - Born in Naigaon, Satara district.

1840 - Savitribai was married to twelve-year-old Jyotirao Phule at the age of nine.

1841 - After her marriage to Jyotiba Phule, she started her education under the guidence of her husband.

1847 - Savitribai Phule along with Sagunbai startde school in Maharwada on 1st may 1847, initially 8-9 girls were enrolled but within year strength reached to 40-45.

1848 - They started the first Indian girls school in Pune, at Bhidewada.

1849 - Savitribai Phule as a teacher in the school at the time when upper caste orthodox people used to look down wasn't easy and many times they used to pelt stones and throw dung on her.

1851 - Savitribai and Jyotirao Phule were running three differnt schools for girls in Pune.

1852 - On 16th November, Phule family was honoured by the british government for their works in the field of education and Savitribai was declared as the best teacher. And she also started Mahila Seva Mandal.

1868 - Savitribai Phule and Mahatma Jyotirao Phule open the well in their house for untouchables.

1873 - When Jyotiba Phule established the Satya Shodhak Samaj, Savitribai became the head of the women's section included ninety female members.

1897 - Dies at the age of 66 while serving a plague patient.
